Lesson Activity Plan

Grade: 5th Grade | Subject: Science | Topic: Solar System
Duration: 45 minutes | Date: August 18, 2025

Learning Objectives

Students will:

  • Create and use a scale model to demonstrate relative distances between planets
  • Compare and contrast characteristics of inner and outer planets using evidence-based reasoning

Materials Needed

  • Roll of paper or string
  • Markers
  • Measuring tape
  • Index cards
  • Basic art supplies

üåç ELL Support: Visual Vocabulary Cards

Provide picture cards for key vocabulary: orbit, revolution, solar system. Include translated labels in students' native languages to support comprehension.

Activity Instructions

Opening Hook (7 minutes)

Begin with a "Walk the Solar System" demonstration where one step equals 10 million miles, helping students grasp the vast distances involved.

Main Activity (32 minutes)

Phase 1: Explore (14 minutes)

  • Students create a scaled model of the Solar System using paper/string
  • Mark planetary positions using provided scale (1 inch = 10 million miles)
  • Label each planet with basic characteristics on index cards

üß† Advanced Extension: Gravitational Force Calculations

Challenge advanced students to calculate and compare gravitational forces on different planets using the formula F = ma. Provide planet mass data and have them determine how their weight would change.

Additional Materials: Scientific calculators, planet data sheets

Phase 2: Apply Understanding (12 minutes)

Students work in pairs to:

  • Measure distances between planets
  • Record observations about spacing patterns
  • Form claims about planet distribution

Phase 3: Connect & Extend (7 minutes)

Students consider:

  • Why inner planets are different from outer planets
  • How distance from the Sun affects planetary characteristics

Teaching Tips

  • Use hallway space for full-scale modeling
  • Emphasize the importance of precise measurements
  • Guide students to connect distance patterns to planetary characteristics
