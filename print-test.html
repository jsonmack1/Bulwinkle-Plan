Differentiation Options

Cosmic Scale Solar Safari

Learning Objectives

Students will:

  • Create and use a scale model to demonstrate relative distances between planets
  • Compare and contrast characteristics of inner and outer planets using evidence-based reasoning

Materials Needed

  • Roll of paper or string
  • Markers
  • Measuring tape
  • Index cards
  • Basic art supplies

Activity Instructions

Opening Hook (7 minutes)

Begin with a "Walk the Solar System" demonstration where one step equals 10 million miles, helping students grasp the vast distances involved.

ðŸŽ¯ ELL Support: Visual Vocabulary

Provide picture cards for key vocabulary: orbit, revolution, solar system. Include translated labels in students' native languages.

Main Activity (32 minutes)

Phase 1: Explore (14 minutes)

  • Students create a scaled model of the Solar System using paper/string
  • Mark planetary positions using provided scale (1 inch = 10 million miles)
  • Label each planet with basic characteristics on index cards

ðŸ§  Challenge Extension: Calculate Gravity

Advanced students can calculate and compare gravitational forces on different planets using the formula F = ma.

Assessment Ideas

  • Monitor accuracy of scale models
  • Listen for evidence-based explanations during discussions
  • Check completed CER responses for understanding

Teaching Tips

  • Use hallway space for full-scale modeling
  • Emphasize the importance of precise measurements
  • Guide students to connect distance patterns to planetary characteristics
