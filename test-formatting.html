Teaching Insights & Discussion Starters

Frame this as: "Imagine Earth's surface as a giant puzzle, where massive pieces are constantly moving and interacting - that's plate tectonics in action!"

Learning Objectives

Students will:

  • Model and explain how tectonic plate interactions cause major geological events
  • Support claims about plate movement with evidence from observations

Materials Needed

  • Large paper sheets
  • Colored markers
  • Foam pieces
  • Rulers
  • Clear plastic bins

Activity Instructions

Opening Hook (8 minutes)

Show time-lapse animation of continental drift, then have students sketch what they think Earth looked like 200 million years ago.

Phase 1: Explore (15 minutes)

Students create plate boundary models using foam crackers on foam

  • Record observations of different types of movement
  • Identify convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries
  • Document what happens at each boundary type
